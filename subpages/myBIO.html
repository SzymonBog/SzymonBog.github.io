I don't expect anyone to know/remember me. Even if you think that you know/remember me you probably don't know the real me. I've changed a lot since 2017 and I didn't want to show it. I think that I was afraid to be rejected. But let's start from the begining.

Childhood

To be honest I can barely remember my childhood, maybe because nothing special happened at that time or maybe because my bad memories replaced the good ones or because my childhood ended when I was 14 years old. The only things I remember from those times are certain events with friends, including one school trip in 6-th grade. That's it. When I think about this, it's kinda sad.

Terminal illness

At the end of January 2017 I started seeing double. Soon I went to the hospital where I underwent a brain tomography and then a brain MRI. It turned out that I had a brain tumor. I was transported to another hospital where my fight for life began. On February 21, 2017, my skull was opened and my old/normal life ended. The surgery was successful and the tumor was removed. I was on painkillers for a week after the surgery and I don't remember anything from that week, maybe that's for the better. After that I went through 4 cycles of chemotherapy, 3 days each. After the 2nd or 3rd cycle of chemotherapy, I probably became depressed, I felt like I was a burden to my family, I even considered committing suicide. Suicidal thoughts didn't last very long, but the fact that they appeared when I was 14 shows how much this event destroyed my psyche. But my psyche was negatively affected not only by the experience but also by what I saw there. In the hospital where I was treated, I saw dozens, if not hundreds of children, with scars on their heads from surgery, without hair from chemotherapy, aged from about 6 to 16 years. Seeing this, I began to wonder what kind of all-powerful, all-knowing and all-good god could allow this, and I came to the conclusion that such a god does not exist. If there is a god, I am sure that he is not all-good, all-powerful or all-knowing, because theoretically if he was, he would not allow something like this to happen to children who have done nothing wrong. After the last chemo, the only thing left was radiotherapy(the best part of my treatment). Radiotherapy lasted for 5 weeks, after which the treatment ended. Only one of my friends kept in touch with me and supported me, and the rest of my friends left me and forgot about me. All of this made me feel useless/expendable, which caused me to have trust issues and withdraw from society. I lost a lot of friends because of all this. But, to be honest, I'm kinda happy that this happened to me, because I've learned a lot about my 'friends' and about myself. I learned that only in the face of death can you find out how much you mean to your friends and who your true friend is.
